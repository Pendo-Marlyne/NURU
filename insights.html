The Hidden Cost of Delayed Treatment

Economic Loss

Delayed care costs East African households KSh 1.2 trillion annually in lost income & productivity (Nation Africa 2025).

Catastrophic health spending affects 32% of families — rural areas hit hardest.

Mortality & Morbidity

42% higher mortality in delayed COVID cases; 28% increase in cancer mortality from late presentation (PMC studies).

Every month of delay in transplant cases reduces survival odds by 12–18%.

Systemic Strain

Urban hospitals operate at 65–120% capacity due to backlog; rural facilities refer 70% of complex cases due to funding uncertainty.

Result: higher overall system cost and poorer health outcomes.

Healthcare Access Landscape – 2026

Rural Access

Only 18% of rural Kenyans live within 5 km of a Level 4+ facility (MoH 2025).

Transport + funding barriers cause 63% of rural patients to delay or forgo care.

Urban Hospital Capacity

Nairobi hospitals average 85–120% occupancy for high-acuity beds (oncology, ICU, transplant).

Funding delays force 35% of complex cases to private facilities or abroad.

Affordability Trends

Catastrophic spending affects 1 in 3 households seeking complex care.
